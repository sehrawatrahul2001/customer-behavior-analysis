**Customer Behavior & Revenue Insights Analysis**

**Rahul Sehrawat**

## Business Context

Retail businesses collect customer-level data across orders, categories, reviews, promotions, and fulfillment choices. The problem is rarely data availability. The real problem is turning that information into a practical view of who drives revenue, what behavior signals loyalty, and where commercial action should be focused first.

From an operations standpoint, customer data becomes valuable only when it helps the business decide how to retain stronger customers, where to improve experience, and which categories deserve more attention.

## Problem Statement

The business lacks a structured way to answer:

- which customer groups contribute the strongest revenue
- whether subscription behavior signals better customer value
- which categories deserve greater focus
- how shipping, discounts, and payment behavior affect spending quality
- where retention and growth efforts should be concentrated

Without that clarity, marketing and retention decisions stay broad, reactive, and less efficient than they should be.

## Analytical Objective

This project was built to create a decision-ready customer revenue view by:

1. cleaning and standardizing raw customer transaction data
2. engineering business-friendly features for segmentation and behavior analysis
3. validating SQL outputs through a reproducible SQLite workflow
4. translating the findings into retention, category, and targeting recommendations

## Scope

The analysis covers:

- customer demographics
- purchase amount and previous purchase history
- subscription behavior
- product and category demand
- discount, shipping, and payment patterns
- location-level revenue contribution

## Stakeholder Value

This project is useful for retail leadership, customer growth teams, category managers, and analysts who need a clearer view of revenue concentration and customer quality. It is also designed to show how Rahul Sehrawat brings operational thinking into analytics work by framing outputs around business action rather than technical activity alone.
