# Dashboard Brief

## Purpose

The dashboard is designed as a retail leadership view of customer revenue behavior. It connects customer segments, category demand, subscription engagement, and location performance so decisions can move from reporting to action.

## Core KPIs

- **Total Revenue:** Total customer purchase value captured in the dataset
- **Average Order Value:** Average spend per transaction
- **Average Review Rating:** Quality and satisfaction proxy
- **Subscriber Revenue Share:** Revenue contribution from subscribed customers
- **High-Value Customer Count:** Customers classified as high-value in the engineered segmentation

## Recommended Views

1. Revenue by category
2. Revenue by age group and gender
3. Subscription vs non-subscription spend
4. Top locations by revenue and subscriber share
5. Customer lifetime segment mix
6. Shipping type and payment method performance

## Management Takeaways

- Retention should be prioritized because repeat and high-value customers create outsized revenue impact.
- Subscription-linked customers deserve focused communication and loyalty investment.
- Category strength is concentrated enough to guide promotions and assortment planning more precisely.
- Location-level differences support targeted rather than broad commercial action.
