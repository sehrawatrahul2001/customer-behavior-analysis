# Project Storyline

## Problem

Retail teams often hold customer data without a clear view of which behaviors actually improve revenue quality, retention, and category performance. The business can see transactions, but not the customer patterns that should shape targeting, loyalty, and growth decisions.

## Approach

Rahul Sehrawat approached the project from an operations-led business perspective: clean the raw dataset in Python, create fields that make customer behavior commercially interpretable, validate the SQL layer against SQLite, and package the findings as a leadership-ready revenue review.

## Key Insights

- Repeat and high-value customers contribute a disproportionate share of revenue.
- Subscribers show stronger long-term commercial importance than a simple order count suggests.
- Category demand is concentrated enough to support sharper merchandising and campaign focus.
- Discounts influence behavior, but the highest-value action comes from selective targeting rather than blanket offers.
- Location and shipping patterns reveal practical opportunities for localized customer strategy.

## Business Impact

This project demonstrates how customer analytics can support retention planning, category prioritization, and more focused commercial execution. It also reflects Rahul Sehrawat's transition into analytics by combining operational context with business-first analysis.
